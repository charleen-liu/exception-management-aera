SCM Exception Management — 3-Minute Demo Script
================================================
Presenter: Kate / Sarah (Supply Chain Buyer, ACME Inc.)
Prototype: exception_management_card.html → exception_detail.html → forecast_detail.html


OPENING — The Problem (0:00–0:20)
----------------------------------
"Sarah is a supply chain buyer managing 60 active POs across 15 suppliers. Every morning
she logs into SAP Business Network and faces the same problem — 19 unranked forecast rows,
4 separate screens, and a spreadsheet. One supplier exception takes 10 to 40 minutes just
to understand. Today, that changes."


STAGE 1 — Exception Inbox (0:20–1:00)
---------------------------------------
Show: exception_management_card.html

"Sarah opens the Exception Management inbox. Immediately, the agent has done the triage
for her."

Point to KPI bar:
"She sees 9 total exceptions — 3 urgent, 1 high-risk auto-resolvable. The agent has
already ranked and classified everything."

Point to analytics strip:
"Exception volume is trending up 12% this week. Volterra Components is the highest-risk
supplier at 88% risk score. The agent has auto-resolved 22 exceptions already — saving
3.2 hours of manual work."

Point to first card (Forecast — Volterra, URGENT):
"The top exception is flagged urgent: Volterra is only committing 60% on Plant US01,
weeks 4 and 6. The agent has already identified the root cause — a raw material shortage —
and calculated $240K in cost impact."

"Instead of 19 flat rows, Sarah sees one ranked insight. No spreadsheet."

Click card → navigate to exception_detail.html


STAGE 2 — Exception Detail + AI Recommendation (1:00–1:45)
------------------------------------------------------------
Show: exception_detail.html

"Sarah opens the exception. The AI Recommendation strip tells her everything she needs to
decide — root cause, business impact, priority, cost impacted — before she reads a single
line."

Point to AI Recommendation header bar:
"Root cause: raw material shortage at Volterra US01 line. Business impact: production gap
weeks 4 and 6. Cost impacted: $240K. Priority: Urgent. All pre-calculated."

Point to Joule panel / conversation:
"Joule has already drafted the supplier outreach. Sarah reviews the message — it's
pre-populated with plant, affected weeks, commit percentages, and PO references. She
clicks Send. That's her first action, in seconds."

"In the current state, Sarah would compose this email manually from her email client.
No system record. No link to the exception. Here, it's one click, fully traceable."

Click Forecast tab → navigate to forecast_detail.html


STAGE 3 — Forecast Detail + Erosion Trail (1:45–2:30)
------------------------------------------------------
Show: forecast_detail.html

"Sarah opens the Forecast view. The 10-week chart shows exactly where the gap is —
purple bars are demand, blue bars are Volterra's commits. Weeks 4 and 6 are highlighted
in red."

Point to Key Figures table:
"The table shows the cumulative erosion trail the agent has been maintaining automatically:
Forecast 100%, OC Confirmed 60%, ASN Shipped 45%. Three stages, one view. No 4-screen
navigation. No spreadsheet."

"When the OC deviations came in, the agent grouped all 17 Volterra PO lines, pre-calculated
delta quantities, and recommended: approve 14 in-tolerance items in one batch, escalate
the 3 flagged ones. Sarah batch-approves in one click."

"When the ASN came in at only 45%, the agent pushed a proactive alert to Sarah — no manual
check-back after two days. She sees the full story the moment it happens."


CLOSING — The Outcome (2:30–3:00)
-----------------------------------
"Let's compare. Before: 19 manual rows, 4 screens, 3 emails to the same supplier, a
spreadsheet, 10 to 40 minutes per exception. After: 1 ranked insight card, 1 connected
view, 1 in-system thread, 0 spreadsheets. Time to first action goes from minutes to
seconds."

"The SCM Exception Agent doesn't replace Sarah's judgment — it eliminates the busywork
so she only acts on what matters. That's the agent-driven supply chain."


================================================
Total: ~3 minutes

Click path:
  Exception Inbox
  → click Volterra Forecast card
  → Exception Detail (AI Recommendation + Joule)
  → Forecast tab
  → Forecast Detail (chart + erosion trail)
